# Nature of Craft - 5P Framework Guide

## Overview
The Nature of Craft module helps you define and analyze your craft's characteristics across five key dimensions using the **5P Framework**: Product, Proficiency, Process, Purpose, and Portrayal.

## What is the 5P Framework?

The 5P Framework provides a comprehensive way to understand the nature of a craft by examining it across five concentric layers, from its deepest meaning to its market manifestation.

### The Five Dimensions

1. **Product** (Outer layer) - The tangible craft output
2. **Proficiency** - The skills and knowledge required
3. **Process** - The methods and techniques used
4. **Purpose** - The functional and social objectives
5. **Portrayal** (Core) - The cultural and symbolic meaning

## The 5P Dimensions Explained

### 1. Product (Any Craft Product)

**Secondary Nature:**
- **Utilitarian**: Functional items for everyday use
- **Decorative Artefacts**: Items primarily for aesthetic value

**Tertiary Nature:**
- **Functional Utility**: Practical application and usability

**Examples:**
- Pottery bowls (Utilitarian + Functional Utility)
- Wall hangings (Decorative Artefacts)
- Furniture (Utilitarian + Functional Utility + Decorative Artefacts)

### 2. Proficiency (Needs a Skill)

**Secondary Nature:**
- **Material Understanding**: Knowledge of materials and their properties
- **Practical Knowledge**: Hands-on know-how
- **Craftsmanship**: Quality of execution
- **Experience**: Accumulated expertise over time
- **Emotional Value Creation**: Ability to create meaningful pieces

**Tertiary Nature:**
- **Skill-based Activity**: Requires specific learned skills
- **Intuitive Learning**: Developed through practice and observation
- **Skill Sharing**: Teaching and transmitting knowledge
- **Craft Disciplines**: Adherence to craft traditions
- **Sensory Experience**: Development of touch, sight, feel
- **Aesthetic Judgement**: Ability to assess quality and beauty
- **Craft Knowledge**: Deep understanding of the craft

**Examples:**
- Master potter (Material Understanding + Experience + Craftsmanship + Aesthetic Judgement)
- Apprentice weaver (Skill-based Activity + Intuitive Learning + Skill Sharing)

### 3. Process (Follows a Process)

**Secondary Nature:**
- **Sustainable Manufacturing**: Eco-friendly production methods
- **Material Manipulation Techniques**: Specific methods for working materials
- **Physical World Interaction**: Hands-on, tangible creation
- **Local Production**: Made in specific geographic locations
- **Network Engagement**: Collaboration and community involvement

**Tertiary Nature:**
- **Eco-effective Process**: Environmentally positive production
- **Non-industrial Production**: Handmade, small-scale methods
- **Community Collaboration Method**: Collective creation approaches

**Examples:**
- Hand-woven textiles (Material Manipulation Techniques + Physical World Interaction + Non-industrial Production)
- Community pottery (Local Production + Network Engagement + Community Collaboration)

### 4. Purpose (To Fulfil a Purpose)

**Secondary Nature:**
- **Prosumption**: Producer and consumer are connected
- **Contextual Lifestyle**: Fits within specific cultural contexts
- **Economical Aspect**: Economic value and livelihood
- **Self-satisfaction**: Personal fulfillment from creation
- **Aesthetic Value**: Beauty and visual appeal

**Tertiary Nature:**
- **Consumer Market Focus**: Oriented toward market demands
- **Consumer Behaviour**: Responds to buyer preferences
- **Community Economy**: Economic benefit to local community

**Examples:**
- Market-oriented crafts (Consumer Market Focus + Economical Aspect + Aesthetic Value)
- Personal artworks (Self-satisfaction + Aesthetic Value)
- Community cooperatives (Community Economy + Prosumption)

### 5. Portrayal (Portrays a Meaning)

**Secondary Nature:**
- **Social Significance**: Represents social values or status
- **Creative Expression**: Artistic and personal expression
- **Cultural and Religious Representation**: Embodies cultural or spiritual meanings

**Tertiary Nature:**
- **Traditional Folkloric**: Connects to folklore and stories
- **Ideology**: Represents particular worldviews
- **Local Culture**: Embodies regional cultural identity
- **Cultural Heritage**: Preserves historical traditions
- **Cultural Symbolism**: Uses symbolic meanings
- **Traditional Wisdom**: Contains ancestral knowledge
- **Self-Expression**: Personal artistic voice
- **Individual Works Conception**: Unique creative vision

**Examples:**
- Temple sculptures (Cultural and Religious Representation + Cultural Heritage + Cultural Symbolism)
- Contemporary craft art (Creative Expression + Self-Expression + Individual Works Conception)
- Traditional textiles (Traditional Folkloric + Local Culture + Cultural Heritage)

## Tips for Effective Use

### 1. Be Honest About Current Status
- Only select characteristics that truly apply now
- Don't select aspirational items in Current Status
- Be realistic about your craft's present state

### 2. Be Ambitious About Desired Status
- Think about your ideal craft vision
- Select characteristics you want to achieve
- It's okay to have many in Desired Status

### 3. Review Regularly
- Reassess every 6-12 months
- Update Current Status as you develop
- Adjust Desired Status as goals evolve

### 4. Use for Training Planning
- Gap analysis shows training needs
- Prioritize dimensions with largest gaps
- Design curricula around missing characteristics

### 5. Share with Stakeholders
- Show Current vs Desired to funders
- Use for grant applications
- Demonstrate development trajectory

## Interpreting Results

### Gap Analysis Indicators

**Positive Gap (+)**: Need to develop
- Focus training and resources here
- Set specific development goals
- Create action plans

**Zero Gap (0)**: Goals achieved
- Maintain current level
- Consider new aspirations
- Document successful practices

**Negative Gap (-)**: Over-delivered
- You have more than desired
- Consider if this is beneficial
- May indicate shifting priorities

## Example Workflow

1. **Complete 5P Assessment** (30-45 minutes)
   - Current Status for all dimensions
   - Desired Status for all dimensions

2. **Review Gap Analysis** (15 minutes)
   - Identify priority dimensions
   - Note specific characteristics to develop

3. **Create Development Plan** (30 minutes)
   - List actions for each gap
   - Set timelines
   - Identify resources needed

4. **Link to Training** (ongoing)
   - Design training modules for each gap area
   - Engage stakeholders (from SAT)
   - Monitor market alignment (from MAT)

5. **Reassess** (every 6 months)
   - Update Current Status
   - Review Desired Status
   - Track progress
