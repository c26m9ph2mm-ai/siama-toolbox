# Quick Start: K-Means Clustering for Stakeholder Subgroups

## 3-Minute Guide

### What You'll Do
Automatically group stakeholders into meaningful clusters using machine learning.

### Prerequisites
- Complete SAT Step 1 (rate all stakeholders on Power, Interest, Legitimacy, Urgency)

### Steps

#### 1. Navigate (30 seconds)
```
SAT ‚Üí Step 2: Management Tool ‚Üí Subgroup Management ‚Üí ü§ñ Automatic Clustering
```

#### 2. Configure (1 minute)
- **Number of Clusters**: Use slider to select (recommend 3-4)
- **Features**: Keep all 4 selected (Power, Interest, Legitimacy, Urgency)
- Click **ü§ñ Generate Clusters**

#### 3. Review (1.5 minutes)
- Check **3D Visualization** - are clusters well-separated?
- Review **Cluster Statistics** - do averages make sense?
- Examine **2D Projections** - verify groupings

### Common Patterns

**Good Results:**
- Clear separation in 3D plot
- Balanced cluster sizes (not too uneven)
- Distinct average characteristics

**Needs Adjustment:**
- Overlapping clusters ‚Üí Try different number
- Unbalanced sizes ‚Üí Check data quality
- Similar averages ‚Üí May need manual grouping

### Troubleshooting

**Issue**: Clustering button not working
**Fix**: Ensure you have at least 2 rated stakeholders

**Issue**: Results seem random
**Fix**: Check if your ratings are too uniform

**Issue**: Want to change assignments
**Fix**: Go to Manual Grouping tab and reassign
